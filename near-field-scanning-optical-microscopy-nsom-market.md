<p><p>Near-Field Scanning Optical Microscopy (NSOM) is a cutting-edge imaging technique that combines scanning probe microscopy with optical microscopy, allowing for the visualization of samples at resolutions beyond the diffraction limit of light. It employs a sharp probe that scans just above the surface of a specimen, enabling the collection of localized optical signals. This technique is particularly valuable in nanotechnology, materials science, and biology, where understanding the properties of materials at the nanoscale is crucial.</p><p>The NSOM market is poised for growth, expected to increase at a CAGR of 4.9% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by rising demand for advanced imaging techniques in research and industrial applications.
