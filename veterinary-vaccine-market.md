<p><p>The veterinary vaccine market comprises several types, each serving unique purposes. Inactivated vaccines use killed pathogens, while live attenuated vaccines utilize weakened forms. Conjugate vaccines link antigens to enhance immune response, whereas toxoid vaccines neutralize toxins produced by pathogens. Recombinant vaccines employ genetic engineering to produce antigens, and DNA vaccines introduce genetic material to stimulate an immune response. Other vaccines might include those utilizing novel technologies or combinations, catering to diverse veterinary healthcare needs and protection against diseases.</p></p>
